About Old Dominion

Back when Matthew Ramsey was a teenager, and falling increasingly ever more in love with music, nothing was so exciting to the blossoming singer-songwriter as hanging in a garage with his buddies and jamming out until a fully fleshed song revealed itself.

“That was just such an amazing feeling,” the Old Dominion frontman recalls with childlike wonderment. So infectious was that feeling and practice to Ramsey that he’s carried it with him to the present day: “It’s so incredible be able to sit in your room with your instruments and your bandmates and feel that chemistry and follow it wherever it goes,” the singer says of the tight-knit way he and his globe-dominating band — which includes singer-guitarist-songwriter Ramsey, guitarist-songwriter Brad Tursi, bassist Geoff Sprung, keyboard-guitarist Trevor Rosen, and drummer Whit Sellers — have forever believed that at the heart of their massive success lies their collaborative spirit and, to that end, have crafted each of their acclaimed albums and chart-dominating singles as a truly cohesive unit.

It’s certainly proved a winning method: since breaking onto the country music scene in 2014, Old Dominion — recognized as the Group of the Year at the both the 2018 and 2019 ACM Awards and the reigning CMA Vocal Group of the Year — have notched seven No. 1s at country radio, surpassed one billion on-demand streams, singles “No Such Thing As A Broken Heart” and “Written in the Sand” earning Platinum certifications and “Hotel Key” earning a Gold, from their Gold certified sophomore album Happy Endings, and headlining arenas and amphitheaters across the globe on their super-successful “Happy Endings” and current “Make It Sweet” tours.

Matthew Ramsey

Matthew Ramsey is the lead singer and one of the songwriters of Old Dominion. He was born October 21,1977. Matthew grew up in Buchanan, VA, where he attended James River High School. He learned to play the drums at a young age before picking up the guitar at age 14. He received a degree in Illustration from Virginia Commonwealth University. Matthew moved to Nashville in 2000 to pursue a career in songwriting, where he met Trevor Rosen and the two became members of the group Old Dominion. He has written several hit songs including The Band Perry's "Chainsaw", Craig Morgan's "Wake Up Lovin' You", Dierks Bentley's "Say You Do", Kenny Chesney's "Save It for a Rainy Day", as well as songs by Sam Hunt and Luke Bryan. Matthew currently lives in Nashville with his wife and two daughters.

Trevor Rosen

Trevor Rosen plays guitar and keyboard and is one of the songwriters of Old Dominion. He was born February 18,1975. Trevor Rosen grew up in Woodhaven, Michigan, and first started learning guitar at age 16. He moved to Nashville in 2003 to pursue a career in songwriting, which is when he met his future bandmate, Matthew Ramsey. In 2013, Rosen signed with SMACKtown Music and Wrensong/Reynsong Publishing. He has written songs for a number of celebrated musicians over the years, including "Pioneer" for The Band Perry, "Fuse" for Keith Urban, "Riser" for Dierks Bentley, "Bringing Back the Sunshine" for Blake Shelton, and "Kill the Lights" for Luke Bryan.

Whit Sellers

Whit Sellers plays the drums for Old Dominion. He met Ramsey in middle school around Roanke, and Sellers became friends with Tursi and Sprung at James Madison University. The group separately made their way to Nashville to pursue music careers, but in a matter of years, the fates brought them together to form Old Dominion. According to CMT, Tursi, Sprung, Rose, and Sellers all worked as Ramseys backing band multiple times.

Geoff Sprung

Geoff Sprung is the bassist for the group. He grew up in Virginia. Geoff, Matthew, and Whit all went to middle school together and Whit ended up going to college with him and Brad. They were in the musical circles together which led to the formation of Old Dominion.

Brad Tursi

Brad Tursi plays guitar for the group. He is a Connecticut Native, but grew up around country music as a kid. He once told 30 Festival, “When I was younger I would always make fun of my Dad for listening to country music, and now I am here writing it. Who ever knew he was so cool. There's gotta be a song in there somewhere”. The songwriter and musician written songs for Kenny Chesney, Maddie and Tae, Frankie Ballard, Swon Brothers, Josh Turner, and Joey Hyde. Tursi played soccer as a high school student in Westport, Connecticut, and in 2015, his song, "A Guy Walks Into a Bar", was certified gold by the Record Industry Association of America.
